About

I consider myself a "classically minded" and conservative developer. By "that, I mean a sensibility that promotes function over form. Visually, I rely on styles that I know work because I have seen them work. But I am most interested in the functionality and the content of the web page. I have always believed that those should do the proverbial "talking".

And as interested as I may be in developing web experiences that attract and inform users, I am even more intrigued by the back-end side of web development. My favorite experience is connecting pieces together in a manner that creates a beautiful whole. Informed by my liberal arts background, I am interested in just about any subject -- and shall prove one of the most interested laymen that you will ever work with.

I was previously a writer and researcher in various forums, and I still write from time to time. As of now, I am preoccupied with honing my coding skills and bringing ideas to life. I recently completed a Web Development Intensive course at the New York Code and Design Academy, and I hold a B.A. in Government and Writing from Wesleyan University.
